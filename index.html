RAPID Hand Manipulation Platform

A Robust, Affordable, Perception-Integrated, Dexterous Hand
for Generalist Robot Autonomy

This paper addresses the scarcity of low-cost, robust, perception-integrated humanoid hands for learning contact-rich and dexterous robotic manipulation, a crucial capability for advancing ''General-Purpose Robots (GPRs)''. To bridge this gap, we present the RAPID Hand, the first affordable, fully-actuated humanoid hand, integrating well-aligned vision, touch, and proprioception to enhance embodied manipulation. The RAPID Hand is designed to balance affordability, robustness, and humanoid dexterity, integrating perception capabilities. Featuring a novel, direct-driven biomimetic finger structure that mirrors human hand kinematics, it achieves a fully actuated hand with 20 degrees of freedom (DoF) for exceptional dexterity and durability. Affordable to assemble and maintain, the RAPID Hand can be constructed at a rather low cost using 3D-printed components and widely available motors. Additionally, it incorporates a multimodal perception scheme, which includes well-aligned and synchronized vision, touch, and proprioception functionalities within the hardware. Built upon RAPID Hand, we learn skills to complete contact-rich, high-dexterity manipulation tasks such as object-in-hand translation and rolling, which are difficult to achieve without human-like dexterity and multi-modal information.
